Canada

11 provinces, 3 territories. Athabasca glacier in province Alberta & British Columbia. High latitude, very cold. Approx. 80% uninhabited, 1,420,000 km² / 26% is tundra & 65% annual snow cover for more than 6 months of year. Country with 8th most glaciers & 5th most percentage of land covered in glaciers, despite being the second largest country in the world.

Athabasca Glacier

Past facts

Losing depth at a rate of about 5 meters. Receded more than 1.5 km & lost more than half its volume in the past 125 years. Nowadays, it’s 6 km long, 6 sq km & between 90 m and 300 m thick. It’s not the only one — glaciers all around the world have lost an estimated 8000 cu km of ice. That can cover nearly four-fifths of Europe’s land area with a sheet of ice a meter tall. Between melting glaciers & many other things, climate change has been affecting our planet, which is why we must work to protect it.

Now facts

The most visited glacier in North America. Standard buses take people sightseeing. One of six glaciers that spill down the Canadian Rockies from the Columbia Icefield. It is on the border of the Canadian provinces of Alberta & British Columbia.
