Canada

Canada is the second largest country in the world, located in the north of North America. It’s a federation made up of 11 provinces, each with their own governments, and 3 territories, which is governed by the federal government. It has a very high latitude, being in the north of the North American continent, just above the United States. Because of this, 80% of it is uninhabited, about 1,420,000km² or 26% of it, remembering it’s one of the largest countries, is tundra and 65% of it has annual snow cover for more than half the year. It has the eighth highest number of glaciers, such as the very famous Athabasca glacier. It is the country with the fifth most amount of land covered, which is absolutely huge considering it’s the second largest county in the world.

Athabasca Glacier

In Canada, you can find one of its many glaciers, the Athabasca glacier. Located near the border of the Canadian provinces of Alberta and British Colombia (Canada was once a British colony), it is the most visited glacier in North America, as well as an unfortunate demonstration of how climate change is affecting our planet. You can go on buses to go sightseeing on this glacier, just one of six that spill down the Canadian Rockies, just one of six where you might notice what some of humanity’s terrible deeds has done to it.

How we’ve been destroying this magnificent work of nature

Humans have destroyed countless pieces of art designed by nature and we’ve seen the effects of it but perhaps no effect has been as clear as our glaciers. The Athabasca glacier alone has receded by more than ONE POINT FIVE KILOMETERS and lost more than HALF ITS VOLUME in just the past 125 years. And it’s still losing depth at a rate of at least 5 meters per year. Now, it’s only about 6km long, covers an area of roughly 6km² and between 90 and 300m thick. And it’s not the only one, glaciers all around the world have been dying at an alarming rate. In fact, glaciers around the world have lost an estimated total of 8000km³ of ice. That can cover nearly four fifths of Europe’s land area with a sheet of ice a meter tall. Between melting glaciers and countless other things climate change is destroying our world. We’ve created this monster, it caused havoc and now we must destroy it to save our planet. But we can only do this with hard work, resilience and a combined effort by everyone, as every little helps.
